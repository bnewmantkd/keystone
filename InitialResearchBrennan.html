Exclusivity in STEM


Resources at and out of school are not enough to prepare many people for a world saturated with science and technology, especially with insufficient interest and accessibility.

Many people in occupations with sensitive documents sent through e-mail do not have a clear idea how it works.

Duolingo and Brilliant are great examples of making learning interesting through a practice called 'gamification'.

Scientific Illiteracy


Even those that aren't interested in a STEM career still need access to fun, helpful resources in science and technology to minimise misconceptions and ussues arising from ignorance.

On the Danger of Not Understanding
Technology
  • Fred Lederer, May 2, 2023
  • Justices, judges, lawyers, and the public at large have insufficient knowledge about how tech platforms work
  • Worse, the public does not have clerks or briefs to explain necessary knowledge
  • This ignorance poses security risks like microphones picking up thought to be private conversations
  • Other risks from ignorances include:
  • Cars need to be taken to a mechanic
  • Sensitive documents are sent through email without knowledge of how it works
  • Flammable or explosive technology like batteries can explode without warning
  • AI and cybersecurity are also talked about extensively, but AI's performance is often cherry picked and its errors are from the operator
  • Without knowledge of how technology works, one can be controlled by hyper-partisanship or exploited by phishing

High-Tech Age
